Did You Know?

Your Health Insurance Has Gaps

Even with good health insurance, a serious illness or injury can mean thousands in out-of-pocket costs. Supplemental insurance fills those gaps — paying you directly to cover deductibles, lost income, childcare, mortgage payments, and more.

It doesn't replace your health plan. It protects the financial side of a health event so you can focus on recovery.

How Supplemental Insurance Works →

Average hospital stay costs $10,000+

Most health plans require deductibles and coinsurance before covering anything — that comes out of your pocket first.

Income stops, bills don't

Short-term disability can replace a portion of your income while you recover — your health plan won't do that.

Cancer affects 1 in 3 Americans

Cancer insurance pays from the day of diagnosis — for treatment, travel to specialists, and the financial disruption that follows.
